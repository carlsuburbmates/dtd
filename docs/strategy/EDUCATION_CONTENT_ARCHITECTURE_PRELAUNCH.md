# Education Content Architecture (Prelaunch)

Date: 2026-05-17
Status: planning only (no content engine build)

## Objective

Build useful, credible owner education that supports prelaunch demand capture while matching remains locked.

## Owner problem categories

1. Puppy foundation and early routines.
2. Reactivity and leash stress.
3. Separation-related behaviors.
4. Recall and off-leash reliability.
5. Household behavior (jumping, barking, impulse control).
6. Multi-dog household dynamics.
7. Rescue-dog transition and settling.
8. Safety-critical concerns (bite-risk escalation guidance + when to seek urgent professional help).

## Melbourne/VIC-specific angles

1. High-density apartment and shared-space behavior expectations.
2. Council/off-leash area realities and etiquette.
3. Climate/season routines for heat days and wet periods.
4. Suburb lifestyle differences (inner-city vs outer-suburban routines).
5. Commute/time-pressure owner routines in metro VIC contexts.

## Guide structure standard

1. Problem snapshot (what owners see).
2. What to do this week (practical, low-risk steps).
3. What not to do (avoid escalation patterns).
4. When to seek a trainer now.
5. How to prepare for trainer introductions later.
6. Local prelaunch note + waitlist call to action.

## Connection to waitlist capture

1. Every guide ends with one clear CTA: join owner waitlist.
2. CTA asks for email + suburb only.
3. CTA promise is updates and readiness guidance, not immediate matching.
4. No urgency framing that implies live marketplace availability.

## Content that should not exist now

1. Live trainer comparison claims.
2. “Book now” / “get matched today” language.
3. Rank-order or guarantee language.
4. Internal gate/state mechanics exposed in public guides.
5. Aggressive conversion funnels that conflict with prelaunch truth.

## Minimum first content set

1. “My puppy won’t settle at night: first 7-day plan.”
2. “Dog reactivity on suburban walks: practical de-escalation basics.”
3. “Separation stress: how to build safe alone-time routines.”
4. “Jumping and impulse control at home: owner reset routine.”
5. “How prelaunch intros will work when your suburb opens.”

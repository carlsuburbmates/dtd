# The Transition Phase

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Guide aim" title="What this guide covers">
  The first days and weeks with a new puppy or dog often feel messy because the dog and household are still adjusting. This guide helps the owner stabilise toileting, biting, chewing, crying, and early overwhelm before those problems harden into daily chaos.
</LessonPanel>

## Sections in this guide

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 1 • 6 min" title="Accidents Need Routine, Not Anger">
      Your puppy goes outside, comes back in, and toilets on the floor a few minutes later, leaving you frustrated and unsure whether the dog is confused, difficult, or doing it on purpose.
    </LessonPanel>
<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 2 • 6 min" title="Biting and Chewing Without Chaos">
      Your puppy bites hands, sleeves, ankles, or clothing and chews furniture, shoes, cords, or bedding, making normal interaction feel painful, personal, and exhausting.
    </LessonPanel>
<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 3 • 5 min" title="Owner Overwhelm Is Data">
      The first day felt manageable, but now every hour seems to bring a new problem: toileting, chewing, biting, crying, refusing to settle, or needing constant attention.
    </LessonPanel>

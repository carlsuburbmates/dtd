# The Social Filter

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Guide aim" title="What this guide covers">
  Socialisation is not about exposing the dog to everything as fast as possible. This guide helps the owner introduce people, places, sounds, surfaces, handling, and everyday experiences without forcing the dog past their coping point.
</LessonPanel>

## Sections in this guide

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 1 • 6 min" title="Socialisation Is Not Forced Interaction">
      You take your puppy or new dog to meet friends. People lean over the dog, reach for the head, talk loudly, and encourage the dog to say hello while the dog backs away, hides, freezes, lip-licks, or refuses treats.
    </LessonPanel>
<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 2 • 5 min" title="Safe Early Exposure Before the World Gets Too Big">
      Your puppy is not fully vaccinated yet, and you are unsure whether to keep them isolated, take them everywhere, attend puppy school, meet vaccinated dogs, or carry them around for exposure.
    </LessonPanel>
<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 3 • 6 min" title="The Traffic-Light Rule for Fear">
      Your dog sees something unfamiliar such as a skateboard, child, umbrella, vacuum, grooming brush, car, statue, or person wearing a hat. Sometimes they seem curious, sometimes hesitant, and sometimes they bark, hide, or panic.
    </LessonPanel>
<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 4 • 5 min" title="Handling, Grooming, and Vet Touch">
      You try to touch your dog's paws, ears, mouth, tail, collar, or harness, and the dog pulls away, mouths your hand, stiffens, hides, growls, or becomes frantic.
    </LessonPanel>

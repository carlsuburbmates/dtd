# Remove Household Hazards

## Situation

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson context">
  The dog picks something up from the floor, steals food, chews a packet, digs near garden products, or mouths something from the bathroom, garage, laundry, or bin. The owner is not sure whether it is dangerous.
</LessonPanel>

## What to notice

<ChecklistBlock items={["Many ordinary household and garden items can seriously harm dogs, including medicines, pest-control products, batteries, xylitol, strings, magnets, and some foods or plants.", "A dog does not need to look sick immediately for the situation to be serious."]} variant="checklist" />

## Common mistake

<LessonPanel eyebrow="What often makes this worse" tone="caution">
  Waiting to see what happens, trying home remedies first, or throwing away the packet before calling a vet.
</LessonPanel>

## Decision rule

<DecisionRule label="Rule for this lesson">If your dog may have swallowed or contacted a toxin, treat it as a vet question first. Do not guess, and do not improvise treatment.</DecisionRule>

## What to do now

<ChecklistBlock items={["Move the dog away from the hazard and prevent further access.", "Keep the packet, label, plant sample, or a photo if safe to do so, and call your vet, emergency vet, or the Animal Poisons Helpline."]} variant="worksheet" />

## What to watch

<ChecklistBlock items={["Vomiting, diarrhoea, drooling, wobbliness, unusual tiredness, tremors, seizures, breathing change, or collapse.", "Remember that some poisonings worsen before obvious signs appear."]} variant="checklist" />

## When to get help

<EscalationNote>
  Immediately, if exposure is possible. Suspected poisoning is not a training problem and not a wait-and-see problem.
</EscalationNote>

## What to record

<TrainerReadiness>
  Tell the vet what the dog may have eaten or contacted, the amount, time, product or plant name, the dog's weight, symptoms, and any packaging or photos. A trainer only becomes relevant later if the dog repeatedly scavenges or raids hazards after the medical risk is handled.
</TrainerReadiness>

# Build the Home Base

## Situation

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson context">
  The new dog arrives home, seems quiet at first, then starts pacing, chewing, following people constantly, and struggling to settle. The house already feels chaotic, and the owner is unsure whether this is a training problem or a setup problem.
</LessonPanel>

## What to notice

<ChecklistBlock items={["A new dog needs a smaller, safer starting area before full access to the home.", "The goal is not to trap the dog. The goal is to make the home easier to understand and easier to rest in."]} variant="checklist" />

## Common mistake

<LessonPanel eyebrow="What often makes this worse" tone="caution">
  Giving the dog full freedom too soon, or only using the crate, pen, or laundry area after something has already gone wrong.
</LessonPanel>

## Decision rule

<DecisionRule label="Rule for this lesson">Start small, calm, and safe. Create one protected base area and rest zone before expanding the dog's world.</DecisionRule>

## What to do now

<ChecklistBlock items={["Create one safe base area with soft bedding, water, and one safe chew or food item.", "Use barriers, a pen, a gated room, or an open crate setup to reduce chaos and protect rest."]} variant="worksheet" />

## What to watch

<ChecklistBlock items={["Does the dog settle faster when traffic, noise, and access are reduced?", "Does the dog become frantic, chew more, or follow people constantly when they are overtired or cannot switch off?"]} variant="checklist" />

## When to get help

<EscalationNote>
  If the dog cannot settle anywhere, panics in the rest area, tries to injure themselves escaping confinement, or suddenly seems unwell or distressed.
</EscalationNote>

## What to record

<TrainerReadiness>
  Record where the dog sleeps, how long they can rest, what happens before they become unsettled, and whether distress starts immediately or after a delay.
</TrainerReadiness>

# One Household, One Rule

## Situation

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson context">
  One person lets the dog jump up, another gets angry about jumping, one feeds from the table, another wants the dog to stop begging, and different people use different words for the same thing.
</LessonPanel>

## What to notice

<ChecklistBlock items={["Dogs learn from patterns. If each person responds differently, the dog receives mixed information.", "The problem may not be the dog's attitude. The problem may be the household system."]} variant="checklist" />

## Common mistake

<LessonPanel eyebrow="What often makes this worse" tone="caution">
  Expecting the dog to understand rules humans have not agreed on.
</LessonPanel>

## Decision rule

<DecisionRule label="Rule for this lesson">Make the human rule first. If humans cannot answer what every person will do, the dog cannot learn the rule reliably.</DecisionRule>

## What to do now

<ChecklistBlock items={["Create a family cue agreement with one word for the behaviour, one response for unwanted behaviour, one reward for desired behaviour, and one management plan when the dog cannot cope.", "Start with high-friction situations such as jumping, door rushing, food bowl behaviour, biting clothing, stolen items, barking for attention, or leaving the rest area undisturbed."]} variant="worksheet" />

## What to watch

<ChecklistBlock items={["Watch whether repeated commands drop, frustration drops, calm behaviours appear more often, and people stop arguing about what to do.", "Notice which rules still break down because visitors, children, or one household member are following a different script."]} variant="checklist" />

## When to get help

<EscalationNote>
  Seek support if household members cannot safely follow the same plan, children are involved and safety is uncertain, the dog guards food, toys, people, or resting places, the dog snaps, bites, or lunges, the behaviour is fear-based or worsening, or the owner needs a trainer to coach the household rather than only the dog.
</EscalationNote>

## What to record

<TrainerReadiness>
  Bring the family cue agreement to the trainer and explain which rules are inconsistent, who is struggling to follow the plan, what behaviours are rewarded by accident, which situations create conflict, whether visitors or children are involved, and whether safety concerns exist.
</TrainerReadiness>

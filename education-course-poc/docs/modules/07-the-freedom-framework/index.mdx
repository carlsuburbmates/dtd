# The Freedom Framework

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Guide aim" title="What this guide covers">
  Freedom should expand only when the dog is ready for it. This guide helps the owner gradually increase room access, prevent regression, use simple household rules, and understand optional signals such as a toilet bell without turning them into a magic fix.
</LessonPanel>

## Sections in this guide

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 1 • 5 min" title="Freedom Expands Slowly">
      Your dog is doing well in their safe area, toileting more reliably, chewing safer items, resting better, and following the household rhythm, so you wonder whether it is time to let them roam the whole house.
    </LessonPanel>
<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 2 • 5 min" title="Regression Is Information">
      Your dog was doing well, then suddenly there is a toileting accident, chewed furniture, barking at the window, stolen items, or restless pacing, and the owner thinks the dog knows better.
    </LessonPanel>
<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 3 • 6 min" title="Signals and the Optional Toilet Bell">
      Your dog stands near the door, scratches, whines, barks, paces, or stares at you, and you wonder whether they need to toilet, want attention, want outside time, or are just restless. You have heard about using a bell.
    </LessonPanel>
<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson 4 • 5 min" title="Family Rules Make Freedom Clearer">
      One person lets the dog on the couch, another removes them, one feeds from the table, another wants begging to stop, one opens the door when the dog barks, and another wants quiet waiting.
    </LessonPanel>

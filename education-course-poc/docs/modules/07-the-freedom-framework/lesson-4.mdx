# Family Rules Make Freedom Clearer

## Situation

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson context">
  One person lets the dog on the couch, another removes them, one feeds from the table, another wants begging to stop, one opens the door when the dog barks, and another wants quiet waiting.
</LessonPanel>

## What to notice

<ChecklistBlock items={["Freedom becomes confusing when the rules change from person to person.", "The dog does not need harsh rules. They need predictable ones around rooms, couches, doors, food, visitors, children, rest, and access."]} variant="checklist" />

## Common mistake

<LessonPanel eyebrow="What often makes this worse" tone="caution">
  Expecting the dog to follow rules that humans have not agreed on.
</LessonPanel>

## Decision rule

<DecisionRule label="Rule for this lesson">One rule, one response, one week. If the rule changes daily, the dog cannot learn it clearly.</DecisionRule>

## What to do now

<ChecklistBlock items={["Write a simple family rules template for the most important areas first: rest area, rooms, couch, door, food, visitors, children, stolen items, outdoor access, and alone time.", "Keep the rules realistic and choose what everyone will do the same way for the next seven days."]} variant="worksheet" />

## What to watch

<ChecklistBlock items={["Rules are working if household arguments reduce, calm behaviours repeat more often, responses become quicker and clearer, visitors understand the plan, the dog rests without interruption, and freedom can expand without major setbacks.", "Rules need adjustment if they are too complicated, children or visitors cannot follow them, one person keeps changing the plan, the dog becomes frustrated or unsafe, or the rule creates more conflict than clarity."]} variant="checklist" />

## When to get help

<EscalationNote>
  Seek trainer support if the household cannot agree on safe handling, children are involved and safety is uncertain, the dog guards food, toys, beds, people, or spaces, snaps, bites, lunges, or blocks access, becomes distressed when rules are applied, or the owner needs coaching to make a realistic household plan. Seek vet advice if rule problems appear after sudden behaviour change or the dog reacts to being touched, moved, or woken with possible pain, illness, sleep disruption, or confusion involved.
</EscalationNote>

## What to record

<TrainerReadiness>
  Bring the family rules template to the trainer and record which rules are unclear, who responds differently, which behaviours are being rewarded by accident, whether children or visitors are involved, whether guarding or fear is present, whether the dog has safe rest areas, and whether the behaviour changed suddenly.
</TrainerReadiness>

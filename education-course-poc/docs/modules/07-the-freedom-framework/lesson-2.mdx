# Regression Is Information

## Situation

<LessonPanel eyebrow="Lesson context">
  Your dog was doing well, then suddenly there is a toileting accident, chewed furniture, barking at the window, stolen items, or restless pacing, and the owner thinks the dog knows better.
</LessonPanel>

## What to notice

<ChecklistBlock items={["Regression usually means something changed, such as access level, supervision, rest, routine, visitors, new sounds, stress, illness, or tempting items.", "The setup may no longer match the dog's current ability."]} variant="checklist" />

## Common mistake

<LessonPanel eyebrow="What often makes this worse" tone="caution">
  Responding with punishment instead of investigation by yelling, scolding, forcing confinement angrily, changing the whole routine again, or ignoring possible health changes.
</LessonPanel>

## Decision rule

<DecisionRule label="Rule for this lesson">When regression appears, reduce difficulty and look for the pattern before blaming the dog.</DecisionRule>

## What to do now

<ChecklistBlock items={["Use a 48-hour regression reset: reduce access by one level, increase supervision, add more toilet opportunities, restore planned rest, remove tempting items, use safe chews and enrichment, and track when the issue happens.", "Contact the vet if the change is sudden, physical, or illness-related, and do not treat the smaller setup as punishment."]} variant="worksheet" />

## What to watch

<ChecklistBlock items={["Look for changes in routine, sleep, appetite, toileting, visitors, home layout, new rooms, outdoor stress, weather, construction noise, household conflict, work schedule, and health signs.", "Notice what improves when freedom is reduced again and what remains unstable."]} variant="checklist" />

## When to get help

<EscalationNote>
  Seek vet advice if regression is sudden or paired with increased urination, diarrhoea, vomiting, lethargy, pain signs, limping, confusion, appetite change, sleep disruption, sudden irritability, or defensive handling reactions. Seek trainer support if regression continues despite reducing difficulty, the dog becomes unsafe, guarding, biting, or panic appears, the owner cannot identify the pattern, or the household cannot maintain one plan.
</EscalationNote>

## What to record

<TrainerReadiness>
  Record what was going well before, what changed, when regression started, location, time of day, supervision level, rest pattern, toileting pattern, health signs, and what improved when freedom was reduced.
</TrainerReadiness>
